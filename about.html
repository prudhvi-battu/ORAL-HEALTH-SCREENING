About Oral Health

Oral health involves taking care of your mouth, teeth, and gums to prevent common issues like tooth decay, bad breath, and gum disease.

About Caries

Dental caries, or cavities, occur when bacteria in the mouth produce acids that erode the enamel. Proper brushing, flossing, and regular check-ups can prevent caries.

About Calculus

Calculus (tartar) is hardened plaque on the teeth. It can cause gum irritation and more severe periodontal issues if left untreated. Professional cleanings are essential.

About Gingivitis

Gingivitis is inflammation of the gums due to plaque buildup. Symptoms include redness, swelling, and bleeding. Early intervention can prevent serious gum disease.

Precautions: Maintain a consistent oral hygiene routine: brush twice a day, floss daily, and limit sugary foods. Regular dentist visits help detect issues early.

Motivation: Taking care of your mouth not only ensures a beautiful smile but also prevents health complications. If you notice any signs like redness, swelling, or pain, don’t delay—consult a dentist.
